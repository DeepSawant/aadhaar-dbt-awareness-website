Key Difference at a Glance

Aadhaar-linked Account

  • Your bank has your Aadhaar number mapped to the account.
  • Useful for KYC and general verification.
  • Not sufficient for receiving DBT benefits by itself.

DBT-enabled Aadhaar-seeded Account

  • Aadhaar is seeded and DBT is enabled for benefit transfers.
  • The account is marked as the primary for DBT in NPCI mapper.
  • Required for receiving government benefits via Direct Benefit Transfer.
What is the NPCI mapper?

The NPCI (National Payments Corporation of India) maintains a secure mapper that links your Aadhaar to a single bank account designated to receive DBT credits. If your account is not correctly mapped, DBT credits may fail or go to a different account.

How to Enable DBT on Your Bank Account

  1. Confirm Aadhaar Seeding

    Check with your bank or mobile banking app that your Aadhaar is seeded with your chosen bank account.

  2. Set DBT Receiving Account

    Ask the bank to mark this account as your primary DBT account in the NPCI mapper.

  3. Verify Status

    After a few days, re-check status via bank channels. You can also verify DBT credit notifications when benefits are disbursed.

  4. Keep Your KYC Updated

    Ensure PAN, Aadhaar, mobile number, and address are up to date to avoid DBT delays.
